Peculiar Phrases from Around the World

  1. "Not my circus, not my monkeys." (Polish)

    Literal: It's not my circus, it's not my monkeys.

    Meaning: That's not my problem.

    «Δεν με αφορά.»

  2. "To have a monkey on one's back." (English)

    Literal: A monkey clinging to you.

    Meaning: A bad habit or addiction you can't shake off.

    «Έχει ένα κόλλημα.»

  3. "Swallowing camels while straining at gnats." (Swedish)

    Literal: Worrying about tiny things but ignoring big ones.

    Meaning: Missing the forest for the trees.

    «Χάνει το δάσος για το δέντρο.»

  4. "To have tomatoes on your eyes." (German)

    Literal: Seeing the world through tomatoes.

    Meaning: Being oblivious to what's right in front of you.

    «Έχει/Φοράει παρωπίδες.»

  5. "To slide in on a shrimp sandwich." (Swedish)

    Literal: Someone who just floats in on shrimp.

    Meaning: A spoiled person who has it too easy.

    «Τα βρίσκει όλα έτοιμα.»

  6. "There is no cow on the ice." (Swedish)

    Literal: Relax-no cow is stranded on the ice.

    Meaning: Everything's fine, no need to worry.

    «Μην σκας.»

  7. "Not the sharpest knife in the drawer." (English)

    Literal: A dull kitchen knife.

    Meaning: Someone not very smart.

    «Δεν κόβει το μυαλό του.»

  8. "To have long hands." (Arabic)

    Literal: Someone with very long hands.

    Meaning: A thief, or someone who takes what isn't theirs.

    «Είναι μακρυχέρης.»

  9. "To give someone pumpkins." (Spanish)

    Literal: Handing pumpkins to someone.

    Meaning: To reject someone romantically.

    «Του/Της έριξε χυλόπιτα.»

  10. "To jump from the rooster to the donkey." (French)

    Literal: Leaping from rooster to donkey.

    Meaning: Changing subjects abruptly in conversation.

    «Πηδάει από το ένα θέμα στο άλλο.»

  11. "To buy a cat in a sack." (German)

    Literal: Purchasing a hidden cat.

    Meaning: Being tricked into buying something you can't see.

    «Αγόρασε γουρούνι στο σακί.»

  12. "As clear as dumpling broth." (Russian)

    Literal: Totally unclear soup.

    Meaning: When something is confusing or murky.

    «Είναι θολά τα νερά.»

  13. "To have a face like a dropped pie." (Australian English)

    Literal: A pie fallen flat on the floor.

    Meaning: Someone not conventionally attractive.

    No direct idiomatic equivalent.

  14. "To have a frog in your throat." (English, but shared worldwide)

    Literal: A frog stuck in your throat.

    Meaning: Being hoarse or unable to speak clearly.

    «Είναι βραχνιασμένος.»

  15. "When the chickens have teeth." (French)

    Literal: Chickens growing teeth.

    Meaning: Something impossible, like "when pigs fly."

    «Του Αγίου Ποτέ.»

  16. "To ride as if you stole it." (Finnish)

    Literal: Riding like a thief.

    Meaning: Doing something recklessly fast.

    «Πάει σφαίρα.»

  17. "To be as pleased as Punch." (British English)

    Literal: Referring to Punch (of Punch and Judy fame).

    Meaning: Extremely happy with oneself, smugly so.

    «Πλέει σε πελάγη ευτυχίας.»

  18. "The cat's jump." (Japanese - 猫の跳躍)

    Literal: The leap of a cat.

    Meaning: A short distance, or a quick little detour.

    «Είναι δύο βήματα από εδώ.»

30 Typical Greek Phrases

  1. «Περί ορέξεως, κολοκυθόπιτα.»

    Literal Translation: About appetite, pumpkin pie.

  2. «Κάνει την πάπια.»

    Literal Translation: He's doing the duck.

  3. «Τα έκανε θάλασσα.»

    Literal Translation: He made them a sea.

  4. «Σιγά τον πολυέλαιο!»

    Literal Translation: Slow down the chandelier!

  5. «Κόλλησε σαν στρείδι.»

    Literal Translation: He stuck like an oyster.

  6. «Του ήρθε ο ουρανός σφοντύλι.»

    Literal Translation: The sky came to him like a vertebra.

  7. «Χρωστάει της Μιχαλούς.»

    Literal Translation: He owes Michalis's wife.

  8. «Πνίγεται σε μια κουταλιά νερό.»

    Literal Translation: He's drowning in a spoonful of water.

  9. «Ακόμα δεν τον είδαμε, Γιάννη τον βαφτίσαμε.»

    Literal Translation: We haven't seen him yet, and we've already named him Yiannis.

  10. «Έφαγε πόρτα.»

    Literal Translation: He ate a door.

  11. «Μου έκανε την καρδιά περιβόλι.»

    Literal Translation: He made my heart a garden.

  12. «Τον έπιασε στον ύπνο.»

    Literal Translation: He caught him in his sleep.

  13. «Μάτια που δεν βλέπονται, γρήγορα λησμονιούνται.»

    Literal Translation: Eyes that don't see each other are quickly forgotten.

  14. «Όπου ακούς πολλά κεράσια, κράτα μικρό καλάθι.»

    Literal Translation: Where you hear of many cherries, hold a small basket.

  15. «Θα φάμε κουφέτα.»

    Literal Translation: We will eat sugared almonds.

  16. «Είναι φυτό.»

    Literal Translation: He is a plant.

  17. «Έχει το ψώνιο.»

    Literal Translation: He has "the shopping."

  18. «Άλλαξε ο Μανωλιός κι έβαλε τα ρούχα του αλλιώς.»

    Literal Translation: Manolios changed and wore his clothes differently.

  19. «Αυγά σου καθαρίζουν;»

    Literal Translation: Are they peeling eggs for you?

  20. «Ψάχνει ψύλλους στ' άχυρα.»

    Literal Translation: He's looking for fleas in the hay.

  21. «Κάθε κατεργάρης στον πάγκο του.»

    Literal Translation: Every scoundrel to his workbench.

  22. «Του έψησε το ψάρι στα χείλη.»

    Literal Translation: He grilled the fish on his lips.

  23. «Είναι για τα πανηγύρια.»

    Literal Translation: He/it is for the festivals.

  24. «Τρώει από τα έτοιμα.»

    Literal Translation: He eats from the ready-made.

  25. «Κάνει τον Κινέζο.»

    Literal Translation: He's playing the Chinese person.

  26. «Τα μυαλά στα κάγκελα.»

    Literal Translation: The brains on the railings.

  27. «Άνθρακας ο θησαυρός.»

    Literal Translation: The treasure is coal.

  28. «Μπίλιες θα γίνουμε.»

    Literal Translation: We will become marbles.

  29. «Μας πουλάει αέρα κοπανιστό.»

    Literal Translation: He sells us pounded air.

  30. «Ίσα βάρκα, ίσα πανιά.»

    Literal Translation: Equal boat, equal sails.
